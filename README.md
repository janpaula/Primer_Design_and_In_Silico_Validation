# Primer Design and In Silico Validation - Neurological Disease-Related Genes

## About the Project

This project aims to perform the *in silico* design and validation of primers targeting genes associated with neurological disorders and diseases. It is part of an extension course titled **“Primer and Probe Design.”**

This study focuses on genes involved in neurotransmission, synaptic plasticity, and neurological conditions such as Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD), epilepsy, and Alzheimer’s disease.

The work includes retrieval of biological sequences from public databases, primer design, thermodynamic evaluation, and molecular specificity analysis.

---

## Objectives

- Characterize target genes and their protein products.
- Design primers for PCR and qPCR applications.
- Evaluate physicochemical properties of oligonucleotides.
- Investigate potential non-specific amplifications.
- Compare shared genomic regions when applicable.
- Propose strategies to improve experimental specificity.

## Key Results

- ✔ Primer design with appropriate Tm, GC content, and structural stability.
- ✔ Identification of potential cross-amplification with homologous genes when applicable.
- ✔ Critical evaluation of biological specificity beyond thermodynamic parameters.
- ✔ Development of alternative strategies based on more specific regions of the CHRNA7 transcript.
- ✔ Proposal of a TaqMan assay targeting exons 2–3 of the gene.
